About the DASBEM Project

The DASBEM Project (Dynamic Analysis of Structures by Boundary Element Method) is an advanced computational framework designed for modeling the transient scattering and dynamic interaction of seismic SH-waves within half-space media. Developed by Dr. Mehdi Panji and Dr. Saeed Mojtabazadeh, the project focuses on optimizing accuracy and efficiency in boundary element modeling of complex geological and structural systems.

DASBEM leverages time-domain integral equations, recursive solvers, and arching pressure theory to analyze the dynamic response of embedded cavities, tunnels, and structures under incident seismic waves.

The method enhances computational stability while reducing memory requirements, offering an elegant solution for modern earthquake and geotechnical engineering simulations.
