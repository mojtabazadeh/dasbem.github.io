About DASBEM

DASBEM ‚Äî Dynamic Analysis of Structures by Boundary Element Method ‚Äî is an academic platform dedicated to modeling and analyzing wave-structure interaction problems in semi-infinite media using advanced time-domain Boundary Element Methods (BEM). The project emphasizes efficient handling of infinite domains, accurate radiation boundary conditions, and high-order integration schemes for near-singular kernels.

The research integrates transient BEM solvers, arch-based pillar pressure modeling, and practical case studies (e.g., mine stopes and cavity-induced scattering) to provide reliable tools for geotechnical engineers, seismologists, and structural dynamics researchers. DASBEM aims to be a resource for reproducible simulations, downloadable datasets, and open-source code fragments for education and research collaborations.

Highlights:

  • Time-domain half-space BEM for SH-wave propagation and scattering.
  • Techniques for arching pressure modeling and pillar optimization.
  • Exportable result histories, contour maps, and frequency-domain amplification analyses.
